Katsuhika hokusai

Katsuhika Hokusai has the most recorded aliases in japanese artist history. he lived from 31 October 1760 – 10 May 1849.he produced over 30,000 paintings, sketches, woodblock prints, and images for picture books in total. His most notable work is the great wave off katagawa.His work transformed the ukiyo-e artform from a style of portraiture largely focused on courtesans and actors into a much broader style of art that focused on landscapes, plants, and animals. He was known by more than 30 aliases in his lifetime. It is believed that the aliases he used can divide his life into sections.

It is believed his father was Nakajima Ise mirror-maker for the shōgun. When he was 12, his father sent him to work at a bookshop and lending library. The books are made of wooden blocks, which are polpular entertainment amng middle and upper classes.

He had 2 wives and 2 sons and 3 daughters. The youngest one, Ei, or Oi, later became his pupil and assassistant. His first wife died in 1970s, and took up his second wife in 1997, but she died within a short time too.

He made most of his famous artworks at age 60. He was an aprentice of shunsho, a woodblock print master. Shunsho was the master of the studio, Katsukawa Shunshō. There hokusai got his first alias, shunro. It was then that he released his first work and met his first wife. After the death of shunsho, he started exploring different artstyles, including european styles he was exposed to through French and Dutch copper engravings he was able to acquire.
